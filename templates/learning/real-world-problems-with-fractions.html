Real-World Problems with Fractions

Introduction:

We can solve real-world problems by using addition and subtraction. In this lesson, we will be adding fractions. A fraction is a representation of a part of a whole. The numerator is the value on the top, and it represents the part. The denominator is the value on the bottom, and it represents the whole.

Addition:

To add two fractions, they must have the same denominator, which we call a common denominator. You add the numerators of each fraction, then the denominator remains the same. Reduce the fraction if necessary.

Example: 1/5 + 2/5 = 3/5

Subtraction:

To subtract two fractions, they must have the same denominator, which we call a common denominator. You subtract the numerators of each fraction, then the denominator remains the same. Reduce the fraction if necessary.

Example: 6/7 - 4/7 = 2/7

You Try:

36 of the cards in your deck are Trainer cards. What fraction of your cards are NOT Trainer cards? Please write your answer as a fraction in simplest terms (for example: 3/14).
