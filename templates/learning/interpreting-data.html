Interpreting Data

Introduction:

We can make observations about data displays.

Circle Graphs:

A circle graph is a way to represent different categories as parts of a whole. The more of the circle that a part takes up, the more common that category is.

Example: The circle graph below shows the results of asking a classroom of students their favorite color. We can make various observations, such as red and yellow are the least favorite colors because they take up the smallest parts of the circle.
