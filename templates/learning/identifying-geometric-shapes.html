Identifying Geometric Shapes

Introduction:

There are a variety of geometric shapes. We will learn about parallelograms, rhombuses, and trapezoids.

Parallelograms:

When two lines are parallel, they remain the same distance apart and never meet. A parallelogram is a four-sided figure where the opposite sides are parallel.

Example: A square is a parallelogram. It has four sides, and the opposite sides are parallel (they never touch).

Rhombuses:

A rhombus is an example of a parallelogram. It is special because all four of its sides must be congruent (the same length). It looks like a slanted square.
