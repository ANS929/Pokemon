Comparing Whole Numbers

Introduction:

Two whole numbers can be compared using > (greater than), = (equals), and < (less than) symbols.

Greater Than:

The greater than symbol (>) is used to show that the number to the left has a higher value than the number on the right. For example, 100 > 92. This would be read as “100 is greater than 92.”

Less Than:

The less than symbol (<) is used to show that the number to the left has a lesser value than the number on the right. For example, 31 < 34. This would be read as “31 is less than 34.”

Equals:

The equals symbol (=) is used to show that two numbers are equal in value. For example, 29 = 29. This would be read as “29 equals 29.”
