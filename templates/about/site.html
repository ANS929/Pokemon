About PokéMath

Launched in 2024, PokéMath is an innovative educational platform designed by an experienced former educator. It integrates mathematics curriculum with the Pokémon Trading Card Game (TCG), providing an engaging experience for students in grades first through eighth.

Features:

  • Comprehensive Curriculum
    • Standards-based content aligned with grade-level expectations
    • Structured into units, each containing lessons and quizzes designed to reinforce mathematical concepts
  • Interactive Learning
    • Lessons include knowledge checks to ensure students grasp the material effectively
    • Engaging activities featuring Pokémon TCG elements to maintain student interest and motivation
  • Community Collaboration
    • Forum feature allows all members to interact, discuss topics, and collaborate on learning strategies
    • Encourages peer-to-peer learning and support

How it Works:

  • For Students
    • Explore grade-appropriate lessons that combine mathematics with Pokémon-themed activities
    • Complete quizzes and knowledge checks to reinforce understanding
  • For Teachers
    • Create and manage class rosters
    • Monitor student progress and performance
  • For Parents
    • Track their child's learning journey
    • Gain insight into areas where their child may need support or encouragement

About the Creator

Alex Smith, the creator of PokéMath, is a lifelong learner with a passion for math and Pokémon. She graduated from Trine University in 2011 with a Bachelor's in Mathematics and Mathematics Education. After spending eight years as a math teacher, Alex transitioned to a career in technology. She completed her Master’s in Information Technology at the University of Glasgow in September 2024.

A Pokémon fan since childhood, Alex began her journey with Pokémon Yellow, a cherished Christmas gift from 1999. Although she collected cards as a child, she did not learn how to play until she was an adult. In 2017, she attended a local game store (LGS) in Indianapolis where she learned the rules. Alex enjoys both casual and competitive play, having recently competed at the European International Championships (EUIC) in London in April 2024.

Driven by a desire to create an engaging math learning experience, Alex combined her background in education with her newly developed programming skills to bring PokéMath to life. Through this project, she sought to combine her love for math and Pokémon, inspiring others to pursue their passions and reach for their goals. PokéMath embodies Alex's dedication and vision, reflecting her belief that hard work and ambition can lead to incredible achievements.
